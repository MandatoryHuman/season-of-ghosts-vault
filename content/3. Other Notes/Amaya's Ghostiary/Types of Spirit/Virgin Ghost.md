## Overview

In certain regions and time periods, a woman who died without marrying and producing children was considered to have lived a pointless life. In this case, they would be unable to pass on to the afterlife, doomed to walk the earth as a Cheonyeo Gwisin. Chonggak Gwishin, or "Bachelor Ghosts" are the male counterpart.

## Behaviour and Haunts

- Virgin Ghosts are known to haunt newly married couples out of jealousy, though their antics rarely reach murderous levels.
- Sometimes a Virgin Ghost will haunt a particular person, causing that person to subconsciously grow a resentment that will prevent them from ever wanting to marry.

### Recognition Guide

- These spirits can be near-indistinguishable from humans, as they normally keep a neat and ordinary appearance, but at times they can appear to cry blood, or even be entirely covered in blood.
- Traditionally, only married women would tie their hair up, so Virgin Ghosts would always have their hair loose.

### Associated Hazards

- Illness
- Accidents

## Exorcism and Resolution

- It is thought that burying a body with a doll emphasizing the male genitalia, or erecting a phallic statue in their name may prevent a spirit returning as a Cheonyeo Gwisin.
- Alternatively, a posthumourous wedding between a Vigin and Bachelor ghost can be performed to allow both to pass to the afterlife.
