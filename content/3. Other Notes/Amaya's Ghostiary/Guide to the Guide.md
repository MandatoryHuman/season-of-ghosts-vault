This guide is focused on listing different types of Spirits from real world folklore; this means that Pathfinder sources have intentionally not been consulted, making the guide purposely inaccurate (gotta leave some excitement 😉).

Also note that the Season of Ghosts Player's guide explicitly calls out the fact that it uses the term "spirit" and "ghost" interchangeably, even though different cultures define them differently, and as such I will be doing the same.

# Notes on Cultural Perceptions of Spirits

Most (if not all) of the cultures I will touch upon here have existed for over a millennium, and over that time they have shifted, mixed, and changed. The things stated below are a single snapshot, not comprehensive definitions.

## Chinese Ghosts

Most Chinese ghosts are the lingering spirits of a person who has died, portrayed as vengeful spirits who will harm the living if provoked. The spirits of the dead - primarily of ancestors - can also be contacted via a medium, and can help the living when properly respected.
