## Details

Lung Wa were the defacto rulers of Tian Xia from 6642 IC to 7106 IC. Their rule was stable, yet brutal. Its harsh and unforgiving taxes fuelled its military and corrupt officials alike. Failure to pay was penalized by forced labour or enlistment into an army led mostly by commanders who’d bought their stations. Even wealth brought in by the rediscovery of the maps of [Aganhei](https://pathfinderwiki.com/wiki/Path_of_Aganhei) didn’t improve life for the majority of citizens. Whenever unrest grew, Lung Wa leaders deflected blame on ancestral minorities, strictly controlling or erasing any communities, practices, or cultural identities perceived as a threat.

Reliance on local folk heroes emerged from the distrust people had toward the government. At first, these heroes were mostly lone vigilantes who later accepted disciples, formed schools, or passed their skills on to their children. These schools, families, and societies—plus the occasional solitary prodigy—became an avenue for common folk to seek help, right wrongs, or settle differences. Lung Wa officials had mixed feelings toward this force that both pacified the populace and was too popular to move against. Both sides wisely avoided each other, though some families carefully lived in both worlds, providing Lung Wa with just enough competence that its borders could expand as they intimidated, harassed, or invaded nearby territories.

The collapse of the empire of Lung Wa is a less immediate concern to Willowshore, even though on a larger regional scale, this political upheaval is entirely reshaping Tian Xia. While [[Shenmen]]’s rule under Lung Wa ended in 7106, the fact that small towns like [[Willowshore]] have traditionally been largely ignored and left to their own devices under governors installed by Lung Wa edicts has largely sheltered Willowshore from ramifications, even two years after the empire’s collapse.
